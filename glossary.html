BASQUIAT, JEAN-MICHEL 1960 - 1988

One of the iconic African-American artists of the 20th century, Jean-Michel Basquiat's Neo-Expressionist paintings catapulted him from humble beginnings in Brooklyn, NY to artistic collaborations with the likes of Andy Warhol. Though he died tragically young, Basquiat left a legendary body of work that blended themes and imagery various cultures, "high art", and pop culture. Source
  • Associated Artists:
    • Andy Warhol, Jackson Pollock, Keith Haring, Cy Twombly

10TH STREET GALLERIES 1952-1962

The 10th Street Galleries were a cooperative collective of galleries that operated mainly in the East Village in the 1950s and '60s. These galleries, run entirely by artists, served as a more progressive and experimental alternative to the Midtown arts scene. Among these galleries, some of the most well known were The Tanager Gallery, The Hansa Gallery, The Phoenix Gallery and the Area Gallery. Although most of the 10th Street galleries have since shuttered, the Phoenix Gallery has continued its operations. It is, however, no longer located in the East Village.

  • Associated Artists:
    Alice Neel, Alex Katz, Allan Kaprow, Elaine de Kooning, George Segal, Leon Polk Smith, Mark di Suvero, Red Grooms, Ted Joans, Yayoi Kusama, and many more!
