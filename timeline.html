New York's Bohemia and Beat Scene


Relevant Artistic Movements: NY Beat Movement, Abstract Expressionism
Until the mid-20th century the area was simply known as the northern part of the Lower East Side, with a similar culture of immigrant, working-class life. In the 1950s and 1960s the migration of Beatniks into the neighborhood later attracted musicians, writers, and artists who had been priced out of the rapidly gentrifying Greenwich Village.  Among the first displaced Greenwich Villagers to move to the area were writers Allen Ginsberg, W. H. Auden, and Norman Mailer, who all moved to the area between 1951 and 1953.
